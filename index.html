What would you do with...

Every engagement starts the same way: I sit with your staff and ask what's repetitive, what's slow, and what's one mistake away from a bad day. Then I build the thing that makes it go away.

Most of my clients aren't technical, and that's the point. They want someone they can trust to handle the software, without having to think about databases, APIs, or deployment, so they can focus on the work that actually matters.

Generic tools weren't built for your workflow. In the AI era, every staff deserves software that fits exactly how they operate, not the other way around.

The scaffolding is easy. The architecture is hard. AI can stand up a working prototype in an afternoon, but it can't tell you whether your data model survives the second use case, or whether three integrations will quietly corrupt each other under real load. That's still the same craft I came up doing — at internet scale, in production, on systems people actually depended on.
