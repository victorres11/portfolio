# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Warm referrals — people who arrive already having heard about Vic Torres ("VT Sports Solutions") from a colleague, a current client, or word of mouth, and are now vetting credibility before reaching out. They are mostly **non-technical sports professionals**: broadcasters and on-air talent (play-by-play announcers, analysts, sideline reporters), plus team and front-office staff (MLB clubs, college programs, agencies).

Their context: a high-trust, low-volume evaluation. They aren't comparison-shopping a marketplace of vendors — someone they respect already pointed them here. The job to be done is *confirm this person is the real thing and easy to work with*, then take the next step (the intake form / "Start a project"). They want to trust the software to someone so they don't have to think about databases, APIs, or deployment.

## Product Purpose

A one-page portfolio that converts a warm referral into a started conversation. It exists to make Vic's pitch legible to people who can't evaluate code: that he embeds with a staff, finds the repetitive/slow/risky work, and ships the one tool that makes it go away. Success = a qualified referral lands, believes the track record (AdRoll, Datadog, startups) and the client roster (Mariners, Big Ten Network, a mid-market MLB franchise, NBC), and clicks through to the intake form.

It is also a quiet proof-of-craft: the site itself should demonstrate the taste and rigor the service promises. The medium is part of the message.

## Brand Personality

**Trusted, precise, embedded.** The voice of a senior craftsman who sits beside your staff, not a vendor pitching from across a table. Calm authority over hype. Specific over sweeping ("saves the five hours nobody else will build," not "10x your workflow"). Plainspoken to non-technical clients while signaling deep expertise to anyone who looks closer. Emotional goal: *relief and confidence* — "I can hand this off and stop worrying about it."

## Anti-references

This should NOT look like:

- **A generic SaaS landing page** — gradient hero, repeated icon+heading feature-card grid, the big-metric/hero-stat template, stock "AI" imagery, eyebrow kickers above every section.
- **A corporate sports-tech vendor** — stadium stock photography, navy-and-silver enterprise palette, jargon-heavy pitch-deck energy.
- **A crypto / dev-tool dark mode** — neon-on-black terminal aesthetic, monospace-everything, hacker costume. (Vic is technical, but his clients are not; mono-as-costume would alienate them.)
- **An overdesigned agency reel** — scroll-jacking, animation for its own sake, style over substance. Motion here must earn its place and serve the story.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The site is a work sample. Its precision, restraint, and finish are the argument; sloppiness anywhere undercuts the pitch.
2. **Show the work, name the names.** Credibility comes from specific, verifiable proof — real clients, real companies, concrete outcomes — not adjectives. Let the roster and track record do the talking.
3. **Speak to the non-technical client.** Lead with the relief and the outcome, not the stack. Technical depth is available for those who look, never a barrier for those who don't.
4. **Embedded, not transactional.** Every word should feel like a partner who sits with your staff, not a contractor sending invoices. Warmth without softness.
5. **Earned motion only.** A warm referral is already convinced of interest — don't perform. Animation (the play-diagram canvas, reveals) should reinforce craft and the sports context, never distract from the read.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal compliance bar specified by the owner. Default to sensible craft: maintain readable contrast (the existing muted-gray body tones are the area to watch), keep tap targets generous, and preserve the existing `prefers-reduced-motion` consideration for the canvas play-animation and scroll reveals since the audience may view on bright sidelines or older devices. Not a hard requirement — a quality default.
